# Business Plan: Automated Fitness Product Engine

## 1. Business Concept

BarbellBloke becomes a digital fitness product business powered by repeatable short-form content, automated lead capture, automated delivery, and weekly data-led content improvement.

The business should start simple: one TikTok profile, one free lead magnet, one low-ticket product, one email list, and one weekly analytics loop.

## 2. Ideal Customer

### Primary persona

**Beginner Ben / Beginner Beth**

- Wants to build muscle, lose fat, or feel confident in the gym
- Feels overwhelmed by complicated fitness advice
- Has tried random workouts but lacks structure
- Watches TikTok for gym tips and motivation
- Needs simple plans, exercise form cues, and accountability

### Buying triggers

- "I don't know what to do in the gym."
- "I want a plan that tells me exactly what to train."
- "I am worried my squat/deadlift/bench form is wrong."
- "I want to look better but not live in the gym."
- "I need something simple I can follow after work."

## 3. Product Suite

### Free lead magnet: 7-Day Strength Reset

A short PDF and email sequence that gives a simple week of workouts, form cues, and confidence-building tasks.

**Goal:** Convert TikTok viewers into owned email leads.

### Starter product: Beginner Barbell Blueprint

A downloadable guide with:

- 4-week beginner gym plan
- Squat, bench, deadlift, row, overhead press setup cues
- Warm-up checklist
- Progress tracker
- Common mistakes and fixes
- FAQ for nervous beginners

**Goal:** Validate buyers and create testimonials.

### Core product: 8-Week Strength & Fat-Loss Plan

A more complete programme with training, habits, simple nutrition guidance, and weekly accountability emails.

**Goal:** Main monthly revenue driver.

### Recurring product: BarbellBloke Club

Monthly membership with:

- New monthly training block
- Exercise demos and form checklists
- Member-only challenges
- Office-hour Q&A or priority questions
- Monthly habit tracker

**Goal:** Recurring revenue and customer retention.

### Premium offer: Remote Form Review Pack

Customers submit videos using an intake form, receive a structured review, and are upsold into a plan.

**Goal:** Higher-value service that informs future products.

## 4. Funnel

1. **TikTok video** creates awareness through hooks, demos, mistakes, relatable gym problems, and product proof.
2. **Bio link** sends viewers to a simple landing page for the free 7-Day Strength Reset.
3. **Email sequence** delivers the free asset, builds trust, tells the brand story, and introduces the starter product.
4. **Checkout** sells the Beginner Barbell Blueprint and auto-delivers files.
5. **Post-purchase automation** asks for goals, encourages completion, requests testimonials, and offers the 8-week plan.
6. **Weekly content loop** uses comments, questions, and analytics to create the next batch of posts.

## 5. Automation Map

| Workflow | Trigger | Automated actions | Human review |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Content intake | New idea/comment added | Draft hook, script, caption, CTA, hashtags | Approve and film |
| Content repurpose | Video marked posted | Draft email, carousel, blog note, and follow-up video ideas | Approve before publishing |
| Lead magnet delivery | New signup | Send PDF, tag lead, start welcome sequence | Monitor conversion |
| Product sale | New order | Deliver files, tag buyer, start onboarding, update sales dashboard | Handle refunds/escalations |
| Support triage | New support email/form | Categorise, draft response, create GitHub issue if product bug | Approve sensitive replies |
| Analytics review | Weekly schedule | Pull metrics, identify winners, create next sprint tasks | Choose priorities |
| Product drop | Product checklist opened | Generate task list, sales email drafts, launch scripts | Approve claims and pricing |

## 6. Content Pillars

1. **Form fixes:** quick corrections for squat, bench, deadlift, row, press, pull-up.
2. **Beginner confidence:** what to do when you feel lost, judged, or inconsistent.
3. **Simple programming:** sets, reps, progression, rest, warm-ups, deloads.
4. **Myth-busting:** practical takes on common gym misinformation.
5. **Product education:** behind-the-scenes of the plan, customer problems, results stories, FAQs.
6. **Personality:** gym humour, real-life training, brand values, founder story.

## 8. Risk Controls

- Do not promise guaranteed fat loss, strength gains, injury cures, or medical outcomes.
- Put clear disclaimers on downloads and landing pages.
- Ask customers to consult a qualified professional if injured, pregnant, medically restricted, or unsure.
- Keep automated agents from publishing without approval.
- Review all exercise instructions for clarity and safety.
